# Starlink Maritime: Is it the End of Offline Sailing?

The introduction of **Starlink Maritime** by SpaceX has been nothing short of a revolution for the nautical world. For decades, offshore communication was synonymous with slow, expensive satellite phones or SSB radios. Now, streaming Netflix in the middle of the Pacific is a reality.

But does this mean the end of offline sailing? Let's dive into the review.

## The Hardware & Performance

### Speed & Latency
In our tests, Starlink consistently delivers download speeds between **150-250 Mbps** in open ocean, with latency as low as **50ms**. This is comparable to, and often better than, many marina Wi-Fi networks.

### Power Consumption
This is the elephant in the room. The Flat High Performance dish consumes significantly more power than standard marine electronics. Expect a draw of **110W to 150W** on average. For a sailing yacht running on solar and batteries, this is a massive load—equivalent to running a radar and autopilot simultaneously, 24/7.

### Reliability
While coverage is now almost global, "almost" is the operative word. Heavy rain fade, mast obstruction (shadowing), and geopolitical geofencing can cut your connection instantly. 

## The Trap of "Always Online"

Relying 100% on Starlink is a dangerous habit. 
1.  **Hardware Failure:** Salt water and high-tech electronics are mortal enemies. Dishes fail. Cables corrode.
2.  **Power Constraints:** You might need to shut down the system to save amps during a long passage with overcast weather.
3.  **Subscription Costs:** The maritime plan is not cheap. Pausing it when near the coast is common, leaving you offline again.
